Introduction
Music for the Soul

Rock and roll became known in the Soviet Union in the 1960s and quickly broke free from its western roots. According to many music critics, its "golden age" years were the 1980s (especially the era of perestroika), when the Soviet underground rock bands could release their records officially. The great majority of the bands perform in the Russian language.

Rock fans described Russian rock as "more spiritual" than its Western variant - often on the grounds that Russian rock musicians rarely enjoyed commercial success in sharp contrast to highly commercial and manufactured forms of pop and rock emanating from the West. Thus, for musicians, Russian rock was considered to be an art form with a peculiar poetic quality whose primary purpose was to communicate expirience in a poetic way, while for rock listeners, the music elicited a process of engagement between performer and listener, the outcome of which was the comprehension of meaning lodged in the text and the music.

Russian rock...
is alive?

History of Rock music in Russia is paticular and complex. Born behind the Iron curtain as a kind of subculture in the country where the music was totally controlled and suppressed by the government. It grew very popular and an inseparable part of history. There is an opinion that it still rests a part of history and the term "russain rock" doesn't exist anymore. True or not, we have what we have and we love it!
